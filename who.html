Driving Global Investment, Strategic Development, and Regional Cooperation

World Peace and Development Group Limited (WPD) is China’s sovereign wealth investment vehicle, established under China’s Company Law with a capital of $300 billion. WPD’s mission is to diversify China’s foreign investments, maximize returns within acceptable risk levels, and foster sustainable development globally.

WPD partners with international organizations, governments, and private enterprises to drive projects that create economic growth, social development, and regional cooperation. By combining investments with local expertise, WPD supports initiatives that address critical sectors such as infrastructure, energy, logistics, healthcare, education, and technology.

As part of its strategic vision, WPD partnered with Inventure Group for the development of cities, integrated destination projects, industrial cities, and logistics hubs across the Middle East and Africa.

WPD has committed an investment of $70 billion USD over five years to the North West Gate Gargoub Special Economic Zone (SEZ) and Port in Egypt, in partnership with Inventure Group and Chinese state-owned enterprises including China Railway CREEC, Power China & Speco, China Harbour, China Communications Construction, and China Exim Bank. Insurance and reinsurance support comes from Sinosure, ICBC AXA, Mina An, CPPIC, Huatei, and others.

The IGDC International Gargoub Development Consortium, in partnership with WPD and Chinese state-owned enterprises, aims to transform the Gargoub SEZ into a modern logistics and trade hub connecting Africa, the Middle East, and Europe. Investments cover port infrastructure, industrial zones, supply chain facilities, energy, food storage, business, financial, and insurance hubs, multi-commodities centers, education and healthcare cities, and telecommunications hubs.

Through projects like the North West Gate Gargoub SEZ, WPD strengthens regional trade, enhances food and energy security, and creates sustainable economic opportunities for Egypt, neighboring countries, and Africa as a regional hub.
